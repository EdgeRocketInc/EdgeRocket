Top New Resources to Become a Better Learner

Working and learning have become inseparable.

A few decades ago, it was different. Jobs didn’t change very much. Workers received initial training, and they performed their defined tasks for years. Today, job demands compel people to acquire new knowledge and master new skill sets. Working is learning, and learning is working. And you have to be a great learner to be a great performer.

As Stephanie Tilenius (who has worked at Google, eBay and Kleiner Perkins) says in the book Talent Mandate: “It is all about being a learning human being. The people who are most aggressive about learning new skills and trying new things will be the most successful.”

Most career-related courses or books are about a specific business topic or technical skill, such as marketing, coding, spreadsheets, or leadership. But recently, there has been heightened interest in the process of learning — the scientific evaluation of learning and skill acquisition, and the discovery of techniques to help people and companies improve their learning effectiveness. It’s an especially exciting field right now because of recent advances in brain science and online learning technologies.

Below is a list of terrific resources published in the last year — books and online courses — to help you and your team become better learners.

Some of these resources are particularly useful for HR, Talent Management, and Learning & Development professionals. But they are valuable for anyone who wants to learn more effectively and achieve more success.

  1. Learn or Die (book)

    By Edward Hess. Columbia Business School Publishing. 280 pages. $18.

    Excerpt: “I expect that many of you believe you are good learners. This book may raise questions about that…I expect that the science of learning will challenge many of your beliefs about how to learn and how to enable learning in your organizations.”

  2. The Talent Mandate (book)

    By Andrew Bennett. Palmgrave MacMillan. 256 pages. $20.

    Excerpt of Joe Kennedy, former CEO of Pandora: “Now we live in a world of perpetual learning and re-learning…Societally and culturally, we have to embrace the fact that learning is foundational.”

  3. The New HR: 21st Century Talent Management (online course)

    Josh Bersin of Deloitte. On-demand course on Udemy platform. 2 hours, 30 minutes. Free.

    Excerpt: “Companies that have strong learning cultures very directly outperform their peers. They are more innovative, they are able to support customers better, they are more likely to have the right skills they need for the future. And they are more profitable.”

  4. The Thinking Effect (book)

    By Michael Vaughan. Nicholas Brealey Publishing. 248 pages. $19.

    Excerpt: “We are wired to learn. Yet most people are not great learners…Most of the current methods used for teaching are based on old patterns of thought and have done little to prepare people with the necessary abilities to adapt.”

  5. Learning How to Learn (online course)

    Barbara Oakley and Terrence Sejnowski, UC San Diego. Estimated total of 12 hours of content and work. On Coursera platform. Free.

    Excerpt: “Our goal is to give you a better understanding of how we learn, so that your brain becomes a better learner. These insights are based on solid research from neuroscience, cognitive psychology, and dozens of leading instructors and practitioners.”

  6. Leaders of Learning (online course)

    Richard Elmore, Harvard University. Estimated total of 24 hours of content and work. On edX platform. Free.

    Excerpt: “Learning as a social activity, as an engine of creativity and innovation and productivity in society, is going to be exploding in the foreseeable future.”

October 9, 2014, by Peter McLaughlin

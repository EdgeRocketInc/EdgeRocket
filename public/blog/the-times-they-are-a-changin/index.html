The Times, they are a-changin’...

In order to succeed, you must be willing (and capable) to adapt. The rate at which the business world changes increases exponentially every year. Over the past two decades, it has become the practice for any businesses to be successful they must continually change with the world around them. Once upon a time – in the disconnected world – the simple business model worked well... but not anymore. Gone are the days of simply hiring capable bodies to carry out simple tasks and still expect to turn a profit. Now your employees must be constantly and consistently well trained to keep up with the ever-expanding marketplace.

Today, if you want your business to grow you must adapt to the always-changing customer, as well as new trends and innovations in our increasingly digital, interconnected, wireless world. Ongoing Training for employees is crucial for the success of any company or corporation looking to expand. Why? Because the costs of employee turnover will never fall, only rise. Having a well educated, positive, happy workforce is a necessity for a business to grow, instead of becoming stagnated.

Employee satisfaction in the workplace means the difference between profitability and losses. There have been countless studies done on the importance of maintaining a workforce who are not just content with their jobs, but feel appreciated, valued, and actually enjoy the role they play in you company’s success. Happy employees equal higher profits, and higher profits means happy management and very happy owners.

Let’s be honest, no one starts a business with the intention of servicing only a few customers per month. In reality it’s the exact opposite. Every business has hopes and plans to grow by leaps and bounds over one, five, ten, or twenty-year periods. To achieve these goals is to make sure you and your employees are being proactive in the ongoing training and education.

When your employees are treated as important, vital parts of the company while being encouraged to advance their knowledge by taking part in educational or advanced job training, the more likely those same employees will stay longer, bringing their newfound knowledge with them in hopes to grow with your company.

Promoting from within has also been proven to cost far less than hiring from the outside to fill positions. Your employees want to learn and grow. When you offer them the opportunity to do so you are opening the door to a long and fruitful relationship – one that will enrich the lives of everyone in the company.

Every business has room for improvement: Spreadsheet training, New Accounting Software, Online Marketing, I.T., Product Management, Operations, Social Media, and hundreds of other areas where training is available. The only issue any company should have is finding which training is best and most useful. There is no shortage of ongoing educational classes to be found online. The trick is finding which of those courses is best for your company’s employees.

The best motivation after compensation

This question on Quora caught my eye today: “What are some other ways besides money to motivate employees?”. I answered, and decided to post a longer version of my answer here.

I believe that career growth opportunities are the next most powerful motivator after compensation and perks. A company must strive to provide as much of a transparent and meritocratic way of promoting and providing opportunities for their employees as possible. I’d like to emphasize that it should not be just about promotions, because then it’s all about money again. It should be a way to encourage a QA engineer to become a software developer, or a customer support rep to become an IT guy, and so on. Ultimately, most career growth paths mean that an employee would get a higher earning potential, but there is psychological difference, just like the difference between base salary and perks.

I don’t want to be cynical, but I believe that compensation and perks are at the top of the list most of the time. I observed an interesting phenomenon pretty often, let’s say an employee makes $75K working in the office. It costs the company about $3/day to provide free coffee (it maybe cheaper, but I would not suggest the cheapest), so it’s about $600/yr per employee. It’s a perk. If the same employee made $600/yr more, but didn't get free coffee, in my opinion it would make much more negative impact on their happiness. My point is that perks make disproportionately big impact on employees’ motivation and happiness.

How is this related to EdgeRocket? Used wisely, learning opportunities could be both an appealing perk and a powerful career growth tool at the same time.

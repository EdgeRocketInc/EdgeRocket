"I'm not crazy. My mother had me tested!"

I interviewed a couple of hundred software engineers in the past 10 years. I’ve seen a lot of resumes with a lot of keywords, way too many keywords if you ask me. Most or all of them had their education listed in their resume proudly. Occasionally, I would come across professional certifications listed too. Java and Scrum master were the most popular ones in the last few years.

In the retrospect, I had created a mental model for factoring them into my decision making process. I tried to understand why a candidate spent time and effort obtaining a certificate. Obviously, the first thought it that it should help to have it on the resume. It could also backfire depending on an interviewing team’s culture, and level of sceptical cynicism that professionals gain over time. I was lucky to work with teams that would take it as a challenge to uncover if candidates with certifications possessed real knowledge.

Take my advice with a grain of salt, and be careful when listing your certificate plaques on your resume!

November 12, 2014, by Alexey Dmitriyev

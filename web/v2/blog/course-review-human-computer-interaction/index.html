Course Review: Human-Computer Interaction

I started taking Human-Computer Interaction online class provided by Coursera via UC San Diego in late June 2014. I wanted to learn concepts of user experience, and get specific ideas and hits for making better user interfaces. This class is a good example of a MOOC (Massive Open Online Course), in which most of the sessions are video-recorded, and additional resources, reading materials, and students forums are provided. I had taken several MOOCs before, but this one is the first using peer evaluation. The course instructor - Scott Klemmer, who apparently is a renowned expert on the subject.

The course started at a very high level, talking about some history of people interacting with machines, not even computers. It was just right amount of high level stuff for me. It’s been getting deeper and more hands-on every week. We were assigned to do need-finding by observing people, and trying to identify problems that can be improved. I realized a bit too late that I had to find three people and interview, and observe. I managed to survive. Then we focused on story-boarding. I like that the instructor emphasized that it should be done with low-fidelity, using a Sharpie and relatively small canvas.

The next assignment was to start working on a low-fidelity click-through mockup. The tool of choice is Balsamiq. It was possible to use other tools, but I did not bother, because I had played with this tool before. That’s when things get much more hands-on. I had to think of the user flows, and follow heuristic principles to make it right. The evaluation of this assignment is in progress now. The next one will be a higher fidelity prototype. We will finish the UI part of the applications eventually, and I look forward to doing that.

I am enjoying this class. I recommend it to anyone who may not be UX/UI expert, and wants to learn and practice more.

July 28, 2014, by Alexey Dmitriyev
